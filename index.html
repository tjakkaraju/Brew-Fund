How to describe Brew Capital — know this cold
The 10-second answer — use this first, every time
"Brew Capital is a systematic quantitative long/short equity fund based in Houston. We use a rules-based signal process — combining earnings revision momentum, event-driven catalysts, and congressional trading data — to identify high-conviction positions. We target exceptional risk-adjusted returns with strict downside discipline."
Then stop. Let them ask. The pause after this sentence is where interest forms.
The 30-second pitch
"We run a fully systematic process — every trade passes through a nine-gate analysis and a fourteen-factor lollapalooza scoring system before we act. No gut calls. No discretionary overrides. The signal has to earn its way into the portfolio mathematically. We combine three primary signal sources: earnings revision data, event-driven catalysts, and congressional trading disclosures. When all three converge on the same name at the same time, that is our highest-conviction signal. We are currently building our track record in paper trading, targeting a first institutional close later this year."
What systematic quant long/short means — plain English
Systematic — rules-based. Every trade follows the same process. Like a casino — the house wins not because it gets lucky but because it follows the same math every hand for thousands of hands. We are the house.

Quantitative — every signal scored numerically. 14 factors. Each yes or no. The score determines position size. The number decides, not a person's mood.

Long/short — profit whether markets go up or down. Long profits when stocks rise. Short profits when stocks fall. This is what a hedge fund does that a mutual fund cannot. We hedge.
